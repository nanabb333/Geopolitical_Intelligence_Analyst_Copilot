# Example Analyst Questions

These representative questions are designed for analyst workflow demonstration and scenario triage. They are not forecasts, recommendations, or live-monitoring prompts.

Coverage labels reflect the current local dataset:

- Supported: directly aligned with available semiconductor-policy events.
- Partially Supported: adjacent to the dataset but broader than current coverage.
- Unsupported: not currently supported by direct historical cases in the dataset.

## Taiwan Risk

1. What historical analogues are relevant if China announces a large military exercise around Taiwan after a major diplomatic visit?
   - Coverage: Partially Supported.
   - Note: The dataset includes Taiwan-linked semiconductor support and foundry cases, but no direct Taiwan military-exercise analogue.

2. How should analysts frame a scenario where shipping disruption around Taiwan affects semiconductor supply chains?
   - Coverage: Partially Supported.
   - Note: Semiconductor supply-chain framing is adjacent to the data, but shipping disruption and blockade mechanics are not directly coded.

3. What prior events are comparable to a Taiwan Strait escalation that increases restrictions on critical technology exports?
   - Coverage: Partially Supported.
   - Note: Technology-export restrictions are supported, but Taiwan Strait escalation is not directly represented.

## Semiconductor Policy

1. What historical analogues are relevant if the United States announces a new semiconductor subsidy package?
   - Coverage: Supported.
   - Note: The dataset includes multiple CHIPS Act support and subsidy-award cases.

2. How did prior state-support announcements for chip manufacturing compare across market reaction windows?
   - Coverage: Supported.
   - Note: The dataset includes state-support cases and limited event-firm return rows for selected CHIPS Act announcements.

3. What pathways should analysts monitor after a major government funding award to a domestic semiconductor manufacturer?
   - Coverage: Supported.
   - Note: Subsidy-award and support-materiality fields are available for pathway framing.

## Military Escalation

2. How should analysts compare historical defense-related geopolitical shocks with available event-study data?

3. What observed pathways appear when military escalation coincides with technology-sector exposure?
   - Coverage: Partially Supported.

## Technology Restrictions

1. What historical analogues are relevant if the United States tightens export controls on advanced AI chips?
   - Coverage: Supported.
   - Note: The dataset includes advanced-chip and semiconductor export-control cases.

2. How should analysts compare prior semiconductor sanctions with new restrictions on equipment suppliers?
   - Coverage: Supported.
   - Note: The dataset includes entity-list, retaliation, and semiconductor-equipment export-control cases.

3. What observed pathways should be monitored after a major entity-list action against technology firms?
   - Coverage: Partially Supported.
   - Note: Semiconductor and memory-chip entity-list cases are supported, but broad technology-firm entity-list actions are not comprehensive.
